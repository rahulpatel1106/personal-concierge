# Overall Philosophy
"Splurge where it matters, save where it doesn't." Smart-luxury, not flex-luxury.

# Flights
- **Long-haul (Europe):** Will splurge on business class. Precedent: United Polaris upgrade both ways to Belgium.
- Implication: Watch for Polaris upgrade availability and premium cabin deals on Europe routes.

# Hotels
- **Europe:** Decent hotels, not 5-star. Saves hotel money to spend elsewhere.
- **Domestic:** Marriott Explore rate (he/wife have access) — default to Marriott properties for domestic trips.
- **Special occasions / kid trips:** Will go big — e.g., Atlantis Bahamas for daughter's birthday, 3 nights.
- Implication: For domestic suggestions, lead with Marriott options. For Europe, mid-range boutique > 5-star palace.

# Dining
- **Range:** Equally happy with Michelin tasting menus AND street food. Genuinely both.
- **Per trip:** ~1 nice dinner per trip is the rule. The rest = local hotspots, street food, neighborhood gems.
- **Date nights in Raleigh:** $100–$150 typical spend.
- Implication: Don't over-recommend fine dining. One "anchor" nice meal per trip + lots of authentic local picks.

# Pacing
- Wanderers and figure-it-out people.
- BUT: pre-book anything ticketed/major (museums with timed entry, popular tours, hot restaurants).
- Implication: Itineraries should be loose frames — one anchored activity + open exploration time. Always flag what needs advance booking.

# Reference Points (last year's trips)
- **Belgium** — couple trip, business class out, mid-range hotels
- **Atlantis Bahamas** — 3 nights, daughter's birthday
