## Anchor dinner — Saturday night (book now)

**Ava Gene's** — the call. Italian-leaning, vegetable-forward, one of Portland's best. Photogenic plating, great cocktail list, exactly the vibe of a "nice dinner" without being stuffy. Wife will love the aesthetic. No sushi, no Chinese — totally clean. Seats fill well in advance; **book as soon as you know your Saturday night window from the family event schedule.**

**Backup:** Le Pigeon (French bistro, James Beard winner, intimate — but tighter for a toddler). Better for a just-the-two-of-you moment if the daughter is sorted.

Keep it simple. They'll be tired and daughter will be fried from a connecting flight. A neighborhood pizza spot or casual Italian near wherever you're staying. Don't over-engineer Wednesday.

## Thursday + Friday daytimes (kid in tow)

**Portland Japanese Garden** — genuinely one of the most beautiful spots in the Pacific Northwest. Wife's camera will be busy. Toddler-manageable paths, stroller-accessible in most sections. Go in the morning before it fills up.

**Powell's Books** — a block-long independent bookstore. Great kids section, stroller-friendly, wife can browse while daughter is in the kids room. Practically mandatory in Portland.

**Tom McCall Waterfront Park** — easy walk along the river, open space for a toddler to run, food carts nearby for lunch. No planning required.

---

## Sunday — full day before red-eye

**Multnomah Falls** (~30 min east on I-84) — most photogenic thing near Portland. 620-ft waterfall, short trail to the bridge is easy enough with a toddler. Gets very crowded by 10am — go at 8am, done by 10, back in the city by lunch. Wife's best photo of the trip will be here.

Alternatively: slow morning in the **Pearl District** (walkable, boutique shopping for wife, good brunch spots), then back to hotel to pack and rest before the red-eye.

**Red-eye strategy:** Get daughter as tired as possible during the day. Feed her well at the airport. Overnight flight with a 2.5yr-old is what it is — godspeed.

## Cocktail bar (after daughter's bedtime)

**Pepe le Moko** — basement speakeasy under Ace Hotel. Known for old-fashioneds and classic cocktails. Small, dark, great. This is the spot for a nightcap after the family events.

**Clyde Common** (ground floor of same Ace Hotel) — more casual, still solid cocktail program. Good if you want to stay closer to the surface.

---

## Shopping note

Portland is not a luxury shopping city. Wife will find independent boutiques and local makers (especially in the Pearl District and NW 23rd Ave), but this trip shouldn't be positioned as a shopping trip for her. Save that energy for Vienna in August (Goldenes Quartier, Kohlmarkt).
