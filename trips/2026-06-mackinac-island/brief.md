## Getting There — The Play

**No budget airline flies directly to Pellston (PLN).** Best approach for family of 3 with toddler:

### Recommended routing
1. **Spirit or Frontier RDU → DTW (Detroit)** — both fly nonstop, fares from ~$65–120/person RT
2. **Rent economy car at DTW** — drive I-75 North ~4 hours to Mackinaw City (~250 miles, straight shot)
3. **Park in Mackinaw City** — surface lots ~$15/day
4. **Arnold Transit ferry to Mackinac Island** — 15 min crossing

> **Why not connect to PLN?** Pellston is only 17 miles from Mackinaw City but Delta's DTW→PLN add-on is ~$234+ one-way and puts you on a small prop plane with a toddler. Driving from DTW is cheaper, more flexible, and I-75 is a famously scenic drive once you get north.

## Lodging — Island vs. Mainland

### Option A: Stay on the island (recommended for anniversary)
No chain hotels on Mackinac Island. Mid-range boutique fits the Europe budget style.

| Hotel | Rate est. (June) | Notes |
|-------|-----------------|-------|
| **Mission Point Resort** | $225–275/night | Best pick — resort grounds, pool, kids under 4 free meal, open lawns for toddler. Reopens April 30, 2026. |
| **Bicycle Street Inn & Suites** | $217–250/night | 9.4 Tripadvisor, cribs available, downtown location, arcade room. Strong backup. |
| **Harbour View Inn** | $224+/night | 9.2 rating, daily housekeeping, free cribs. More boutique inn feel. |

**For 3 nights (Fri–Mon):** ~$650–825 at Mission Point

### Option B: Stay mainland, day-trip the island (budget mode)
- Courtyard or Fairfield Inn Mackinaw City — **Marriott Explore rate applies**
- Estimated ~$80–120/night Explore rate = ~$240–360 for 3 nights
- Day-trip ferry each day, back before dinner
- Saves ~$300–400 on lodging; loses the island-at-night experience

## Logistics note — No Cars on Mackinac Island
The island bans motor vehicles. Transportation = walking, bikes, and horse-drawn carriages. This is actually great for a toddler:
- Carriage tour of the island = perfect with a 2.5-year-old
- Fudge shops every 5 feet (daughter will lose her mind)
- Flat waterfront is stroller-friendly

---

## What to do there
- **Arch Rock** — iconic, photogenic, short trail
- **Horse carriage tour** — easiest way to see the whole island; toddler will love it; ~$35/adult, kids often free
- **Fort Mackinac** — light history, great views; fine for a quick walk-through with a toddler
- **Fudge on Main Street** — unavoidable and correct
- **Afternoon drinks on a waterfront porch** — every hotel has one
